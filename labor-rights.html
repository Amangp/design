Labour Rights & Fair Wages

Understanding your rights under Indian labour laws for fair compensation and safe working conditions

Know Your Labour Rights

Every worker in India deserves fair treatment and compensation for their labour. Unfortunately, wage theft and violations of labour laws remain widespread problems affecting millions of workers.

At Law & Order Workplace Rights Advocates, we help employees understand their rights, identify violations, and take effective action to recover unpaid wages.

Your Essential Labour Rights in India

Central and State laws provide important protections for workers:

Minimum Wages

Under the Code on Wages, 2019, you are entitled to receive at least the minimum wage set by your state government for your category of employment.

Overtime Pay

Most workers are entitled to overtime wages at twice the ordinary rate when working beyond standard hours (usually 8 hours daily or 48 hours weekly).

Equal Remuneration

The Equal Remuneration Act prohibits discrimination in wage payment based on gender for the same work or work of similar nature.

Timely Payment

The Payment of Wages Act regulates when and how wages must be paid and prohibits unauthorized deductions.

Recognizing Wage Theft in India

Wage theft occurs when employers fail to pay workers the full wages they've legally earned:

Common Types of Wage Violations

Unpaid Overtime

Not paying for work beyond standard hours or the proper overtime rate (double wages).

Minimum Wage Violations

Paying below the applicable minimum wage for your region and job category.

Illegal Deductions

Making unauthorized deductions from wages or imposing unfair fines.

Delayed Payments

Not paying wages within the timeframe specified by law (7-10 days after wage period).
