Indian Legal Framework Against Workplace Discrimination

  • Article 14: Equality before law
  • Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination
  • Article 16: Equal opportunity in employment

Key Anti-Discrimination Laws

  • Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013
  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016
  • SC/ST Prevention of Atrocities Act

Industry-Specific Regulations

  • Equal Remuneration Act, 1976

How to Address Workplace Discrimination in India

1

Document Discriminatory Incidents

Keep detailed records of discriminatory incidents, including dates, times, locations, witnesses, and any evidence such as emails or messages.

2

Report to Internal Committees

Report to HR, Internal Complaints Committee (ICC), or other designated grievance channels within your organization.

3

Approach External Authorities

If internal resolution fails, approach relevant authorities such as Local Complaints Committee, National/State Commissions, or Labor Department.

4

Legal Action

Consider legal action through labor courts, civil courts, or file a writ petition in High Courts or Supreme Court depending on the nature of discrimination.

Indian Success Story: Tech Workers Challenge Gender Discrimination

A group of female software engineers at a prominent Bangalore tech firm documented systematic discrimination in promotion practices, pay scales, and project assignments. After internal complaints were ignored, they collectively filed a complaint with the Karnataka State Women's Commission. The resulting investigation led to company-wide policy changes, retroactive promotions, and the establishment of a robust gender equality committee with real enforcement powers.
